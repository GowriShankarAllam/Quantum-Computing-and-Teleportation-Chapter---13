# Quantum-Computing-and-Teleportation-Chapter---13

ABSTRACT: 
Quantum computing is an emerging field at the intersection of physics, computer science, and mathematics, aiming to revolutionize how we process information. Unlike classical computers, which use bits to represent data as 0s or 1s, quantum computers use quantum bits, or qubits. These qubits can exist in a state of 0, 1, or both simultaneously, thanks to a quantum property called superposition. Another key principle, entanglement, allows qubits to be linked in such a way that the state of one instantly influences the state of another, regardless of distance.

These phenomena enable quantum computers to perform certain calculations exponentially faster than classical machines. Tasks like factoring large numbers, simulating molecular structures, or searching databases could become dramatically more efficient with quantum algorithms such as Shor’s and Grover’s.

Quantum computing is still in its early stages, facing challenges like qubit stability, error correction, and hardware scalability. However, breakthroughs are accelerating, with companies and researchers worldwide racing to develop practical quantum processors. As quantum computers continue to evolve, they hold the potential to transform industries ranging from cryptography and pharmaceuticals to artificial intelligence and logistics, marking the beginning of a new computational era.
